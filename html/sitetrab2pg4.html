THE AMAZING WORLD OF VIDEOGAMES

We've already talked about videogame consoles, but those are used to play videogames. And videogames are like movies, but where you get to control the characters. They can be of many genres, styles, ratings, designs, etc. But not every videogame can be played on every console. The three big consoles have their own exclusive games, that can only be played on their consoles. And then there are shared games, that can be played on two or more consoles.

Games can be played two basic ways: in physical media or digital media. Physical media means the game is on a disc, that you insert into your console. But now, more and more people are using digital media. Some consoles don't even have disc slots (like the Xbox Series S). If you go digital, you can either:
*Download your game onto your console's drive, and link the purchase to your account.
*Play digital games via the cloud, which doesn't require any downloads, but it does need super great internet.

You can play many types of games, like:
*Full games, which are made to pay full price, and come with all the elements. No ads, hopefully no microtransactions.
*Demo games, which only offer part of the game for free, and then you need to pay to unlock the rest.
*Free-to-play games, which don't cost anything at all. They usually have lots of microtransactions, and limited game modes.
*Early acess games, which aren't available for all games. You get to play part of a game before it officially releases, and can sometimes give feedback. Usually used for reviewers.
*Subscription-based games, where you sign up to a subscription service, and get to play games from that (like on Xbox Game Pass).
